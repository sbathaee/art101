Lab 7: Functions

By Kenny Losier and Shayan Bathaee

Challenge

The goal of this lab was for us to gain experience using functions in JavaScript. As usual, we started out by creating an HTML file styled with CSS. After doing so, we were tasked with creating a function that takes in user input and sorts it alphabetically. We also took on two bonus challenges by creating multiple anagram functions, and making the window.prompt() happen outside of our function.

Problems

For this particular lab, we both encountered many problems. This was one of our first labs together that caused some frustration and presented difficult obstacles to overcome. First and foremost, we were not entirely sure what spacing and dimensions the anagram had to follow for the bonus task. Moreover, we were confused whether or not the different words entered by the user had to include the same amount of lettering as well. After that, we discovered that trying to space out a proper anagram proved to be a struggle. Plenty of debugging had to be done in order to complete our function that portioned the anagram of the user's name with correct spacing. There were times when we could not figure out why the anagram would not display and why it would not display the last word after it was randomized. These issues proved to be our toughest and lengthiest to overcome, but in the end, we persevered and kept debugging and googling until we could figure it out together.
